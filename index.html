How I work

Three things I believe about live games

Most of my career has been spent proving these out across very different studios. They're the lens I bring to any player-facing problem.

🎯

Behavior over opinion

The strongest roadmap argument is what players actually do — not what the room assumes they do. I turn ambiguous questions into hypotheses, instrument them, read the signal, and let the game tell us where to go next.

⚖️

Trust is a product feature

Fraud, exploits, and a broken economy don't just cost revenue — they quietly tax every honest player. I came up through game security, so I design for integrity and fairness as part of the experience, not a clean-up crew after launch.

🔁

Live games are never “done”

A live game is a system that breathes — cadence, rewards, progression, economy, retention. I treat shipping as the start of the conversation and build the operating rhythms that keep a title healthy season after season.

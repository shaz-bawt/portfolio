Six years inside Guild Wars 2

Analytics Lead & PM, then Fraud & Game Security Lead · ArenaNet
May 2013 to Nov 2019

One of the most respected MMOs in the genre, lived in from two very different seats. First the job was keeping the world fair. Later it was keeping it thriving.

As Fraud & Game Security Lead (2013 to 2017)

Ran game security and economy integrity for live operations: spotting where new systems could be exploited, recommending fixes, and using player data to curb cheating and abuse without punishing honest players.

As Analytics Lead & Product Manager (2017 to 2019)

Owned the game's health metrics across expansions and seasons. Shipped live events between major releases, with rewards and pacing tuned from real behavior, plus the reporting to track how it all landed.

Why it mattered
  • Protected a loved MMO economy at scale
  • Kept players engaged between big releases
  • Carried security instincts straight into product decisions

Where the instincts came from

QA, Security & Design · Sony · EA · Zynga · Sparkplay Media
2006 to 2013

Before product and analytics, three jobs that still shape every call I make: testing games to breaking point, building one from the inside, and defending one from the outside.

Sony & EA, QA (where it started)

Lead Online QA Analyst at Sony, testing a long run of PlayStation launches, then Senior QA Analyst at EA on the Pogo.com titles (Poppit!, Word Whomp, Monopoly, Slingo). This is where I learned to hunt for edge cases and assume any seam a player can find, they will.

Zynga, Senior Security Analyst

Worked on launch readiness, payment security, and economy protection across FarmVille, CityVille, Words with Friends, and Zynga Poker, using the data to cut cheating and revenue loss at huge scale.

Sparkplay Media, Game Designer, Earth Eternal

Rebuilt the first hour of play for an online RPG: quests, dungeons, skills, rewards, and pacing that made onboarding clear and kept players coming back.

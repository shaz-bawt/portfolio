The story

Why players do
what they do.

I've spent fifteen years circling the same question from every angle a game studio has — and it turns out that question is the whole job.

I didn't take the straight road into product. I started inside the game itself — designing quests, dungeons, skills, rewards, and the first-time experience for an online RPG at Sparkplay Media. That work taught me something I've never unlearned: every number on a dashboard is a person deciding whether your game is worth their evening. Reward pacing, friction, clarity, the shape of a progression curve — these aren't abstractions. They're the difference between a player who stays and one who quietly leaves.

From there I went somewhere most product people never do: the security side. At Zynga I worked across FarmVille, CityVille, Words with Friends, and Zynga Poker — launch readiness, payment risk, cheating patterns, revenue leakage at enormous scale. Then at ArenaNet I led fraud and game security for Guild Wars 2, protecting one of the most beloved MMO economies in the genre. I learned to think like the person trying to break your systems, and to weigh every mitigation against its cost to the honest player.

Security in games isn't a technical-control problem. It's a player-trust problem — and trust is one of the most valuable things a live game owns.

Those years rewired how I see product. When you've watched an exploit hollow out an economy, you stop treating integrity as someone else's job. You design for it. You instrument for it. You ask “how would this be abused?” in the same breath as “how will this delight?”

The natural next step was to move from protecting the game to steering it. I became Analytics Lead and Product Manager on Guild Wars 2 — owning business and gameplay KPI health across expansions and seasonal updates, and shipping engagement events whose rewards and pacing were tuned directly from player behavior. After that I founded the analytics function at Gearbox, standardized gameplay analytics across live teams at SciPlay, and then built product, design, and analytics from the ground up at HiDef for a cross-platform live-service title — progression loops, monetization, segmentation, and the operating rhythms to run them.

More recently I embedded with AAA game and content teams at ZeniMax Online Studios as Lead PM for Insights & Product, and today I lead telemetry and streaming data product work at PlayStation — making sure game teams across the platform can measure player journeys and iterate on what they find.

Design taught me empathy. Security taught me skepticism. Analytics taught me proof. Product is where all three finally do their work at once.

What I'm best at is sitting in the middle of a studio and translating. I can talk progression and friction with designers, cohorts and funnels with analysts, instrumentation and constraints with engineers, and roadmap, trust, and business health with leadership — and keep all of those conversations pointed at the same player. That's the role I want: close to games, close to players, turning ambiguous behavior and risk into decisions a whole team can get behind.
